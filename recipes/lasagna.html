Lasagna

Picture a hearty, golden-brown lasagna fresh from the oven. The top layer is a bubbling, cheese-covered masterpiece, with mozzarella and Parmesan creating a crispy, golden crust. Beneath this, layers of rich, savory tomato sauce and seasoned ground beef intermingle with creamy ricotta and béchamel sauces. Each slice reveals perfectly cooked, wide pasta sheets, tender and al dente, holding the layers together.

The aroma is a delightful blend of garlic, basil, and slow-simmered tomatoes, inviting you to take that first, satisfying bite. Whether served with a side of garlic bread or a crisp green salad, this lasagna is a comforting, satisfying meal that brings warmth and joy to any table.

Ingredients

  • 9 lasagna noodles (cooked and drained)
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook beef in a skillet over medium heat until browned. Drain fat. Add marinara sauce and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Mix filling: In a bowl, combine ricotta, egg, half the mozzarella, Parmesan, salt, and seasoning.
  4. Assemble: In a 9x13-inch dish, layer sauce, noodles, cheese mixture. Repeat layers, ending with sauce and remaining mozzarella
  5. Bake covered with foil for 25 minutes, then uncovered for 15 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  6. Rest 10 minutes before slicing.
